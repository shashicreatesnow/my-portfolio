# Gamma — Portfolio Case Study Plan

> What to show on the portfolio. Business-oriented, solution-oriented. Shows the thinking, not the full recipe.

---

## Positioning Strategy

**What to show:** You're a designer who identified a production bottleneck and built an AI system to solve it. You think in workflows, not just screens. You use AI as a design tool, not just a chatbot.

**What NOT to show:** The specific prompt formulas, the exact agent configurations, the research files, the taste profile details, NanoBanana/ChatGPT Image specifics. These are your competitive edge — the portfolio should make people want to hire you, not replicate your system.

---

## The Story Arc (for portfolio)

### 1. The Problem (relatable, business-focused)
"I was designing 8-15 thumbnails a day for a Hindi edutainment app. Each one needed 5 visual directions to test. That's up to 75 precise AI prompts daily — each one 500-2000 characters of exact specifications. I was spending hours on repetitive work that kept me from actual creative thinking."

**Why this works:** Anyone who's done production design understands this pain. It's not "I wanted to play with AI" — it's "I had a real workflow problem."

### 2. The Evolution (show the journey, not the final state)
Tell it as three versions. This shows iterative thinking:

**V1 — First Automation (~30% automated)**
"Started with two agents: one writes prompts, one submits to Midjourney. Fast but inconsistent — the AI had no context about what makes a good thumbnail."

**V2 — Adding Intelligence (~80% automated)**
"Added research that studies competitors and category patterns before writing prompts. Split generation into 4 distinct visual directions. Then the breakthrough: two AI critics that debate every prompt before it ships — one demands technical precision, the other checks audience impact."

**V3 — The Full System (~95% automated)**
"Connected my Google Sheets workflow so briefs flow in automatically. Built a Design Brain — a mini AI that knows what makes a good design and what doesn't, trained on my taste, my moodboard, and real performance data. The generation agent consults the Design Brain before writing anything. Integrated 3 AI systems (Claude, Codex, Gemini) that delegate work to each other. Built Miss Minutes — a terminal-based visual command center inspired by Loki — to control all agents from one place."

**Why this works:** Shows growth mindset, iterative design thinking, and increasing sophistication. It's not "I built a thing" — it's "I kept making it smarter."

### 3. The Smart Parts to Highlight

#### The Two-Critic Debate System
This is the most impressive and shareable concept. Show it prominently:
- Precision critic vs. audience critic
- They debate, disagree, compromise
- The conflict produces better output than either alone
- Then they collaborate on a "Best Of" that combines the strongest elements

**Why this is safe to share:** The concept is powerful but the implementation details (what exactly they check, the scoring criteria, the taste rules) stay hidden.

#### 3-Way AI Integration
"I built a system where Claude, Codex, and Gemini work as a team — each handling what they're best at, delegating to each other. It's not one AI tool, it's an AI design studio."

**Why this is safe to share:** Sounds impressive, shows technical fluency, but doesn't reveal which AI does what specifically.

#### The Journey from 30% to 95%
Show the version progression as a metric:
- V1: ~30% automated
- V2: ~80% automated  
- V3: ~95% automated
- "My job went from writing 75 prompts to reviewing outputs and pushing creative boundaries."

**Why this is safe to share:** Shows the impact without revealing the method.

#### The Design Brain
The standout V3 addition. A mini AI decision-maker that acts as the creative authority:
- Knows what makes a good design and what doesn't — trained on my taste, my moodboard, and real performance data
- The Prompt Generation agent consults the Design Brain before writing anything
- It's like having a senior art director sitting next to every agent — "this works, this doesn't, try this direction instead"
- The Design Brain is why V3 prompts are fundamentally better than V2 — the agents don't just follow rules, they have creative judgment

**Why this is safe to share:** The concept of a "design brain" is powerful positioning. It says you didn't just automate — you taught AI your creative standards. The actual training data and rules stay private.

#### Miss Minutes — The Command Center
Built a terminal-based visual interface inspired by Miss Minutes from Loki:
- A visual dashboard to see all agents and their status in real time
- Launch agent teams in parallel with one command
- Monitor what's processing, what's done, what needs attention
- Control the entire pipeline from a single screen instead of running separate commands

**Why this is safe to share:** It's visually impressive and shows product thinking — you didn't just build a pipeline, you built an interface to control it. The screenshot alone is a portfolio hero image.

#### Design System Thinking Applied to AI
"I formalized my visual taste, emotion vocabulary, and color system into documents that AI agents follow — the same way a brand guidelines document works for a design team."

**Why this is safe to share:** Shows you think in systems. Doesn't reveal the actual rules.

### 4. The Results (concrete numbers)
- 700+ prompts submitted
- 139+ thumbnails processed
- 37+ production sessions
- 16 content categories
- ~15 minutes from brief to submission (was hours)
- 5 AI systems working together

### 5. What NOT to Include on Portfolio

| Keep Private | Why |
|---|---|
| The 8-element prompt formula | Your competitive moat |
| Exact taste profile rules | Your unique visual identity system |
| FACS emotion mapping details | Specific technique |
| Category color hex codes | Client-specific |
| Anti-poverty rule specifics | Keep as internal quality standard |
| NanoBanana / ChatGPT Image details | Specific tool choices |
| Brightness/variety hard rules | System internals |
| Google Sheet structure | Client workflow |
| Research file contents | Competitive intelligence |

# Ritam — Full Product Ecosystem

> Designed the complete digital product for an Indian news company: consumer app, admin panel, and web app. End-to-end product thinking.

## Why This Matters for Your Portfolio

This isn't two separate projects — it's **one product story told from two sides**:

1. **Ritam App** — what users see. Beautiful, fast, personalized news consumption.
2. **Ritam Admin Panel** — what the team uses. Content creation, moderation, analytics, access control.

Showing both together tells visitors: "I don't just design pretty screens. I think about the full system — the users, the operators, the data flow between them."

## How to Present on the Portfolio

### Option A: Single Combined Case Study (Recommended)
One case study called **"Ritam — Redesigning a News Ecosystem"** that walks through:

1. **The Business Problem** — news org transitioning to digital-first
2. **The Consumer Side** (App) — architecture, onboarding, home, discovery, reading, engagement
3. **The Operations Side** (Admin Panel) — dashboard, content management, editor tools, crawler, moderation, roles
4. **The Design System** — colors, tokens, typography, accessibility (shared across both)
5. **The Outcome** — faster publishing, better discovery, reduced editor friction

**Block structure for the CMS:**
- Heading: "Ritam"
- Text: ecosystem introduction
- Metric Row: key stats (24 screens designed, 4-week timeline, 5 content types, 12+ languages supported)
- Heading: "The Consumer Experience"
- Image (full-width): Hero mockup — phone in hand
- Text: problem + approach for the app
- Gallery: key app screens (onboarding, home, discover, article, news, shorts)
- Heading: "The Operations Backbone"
- Image (full-width): Hero mockup — laptop on stone
- Text: challenge of designing for editors and admins
- Gallery: key admin screens (dashboard, content page, text editor, crawler, notifications)
- Heading: "UX Decisions That Matter"
- Columns (2): annotated screenshots showing specific UX decisions from both app and admin
- Heading: "Design System"
- Columns (3): Colors, Typography, Accessibility
- Heading: "Outcomes"
- Metric Row or Callout: key results
- Text: learnings and reflection

### Option B: Two Linked Case Studies
Keep them separate but cross-link. Each ends with "See the other half" pointing to its pair.

### Recommendation
**Go with Option A.** A combined ecosystem story is rare in design portfolios and immediately positions you as someone who thinks in systems, not just surfaces. Most designers only show the consumer side. You designed both — that's your edge.
